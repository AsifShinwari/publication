SOCIAL STRATIFICATION

The classification of people in a society into groups based on socioeconomic variables such as wealth, income, race, education, ethnicity, gender, employment, social position, or derived authority is known as social stratification. The most significant benefit of social stratification is that it promotes societal order and stability. Because people are aware of their place in society, social stratification may provide efficiency and predictability. Social stratification also serves as a motivator to work hard and contribute to the betterment of society. When it comes to disadvantages, social stratification is the primary cause of a network of disparities in economic, social, political, and ideological aspects.
